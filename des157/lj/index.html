Best Practices for Modals / Overlays / Dialog Windows

I always used to call these windows, “pop ups”, but it is useful to know the technical term, modal windows. Modal windows can be used to grab a user’s attention, obtain user input, show the user additional information, and show information that is not directly related to the content of the page.

I found it interesting that a top highlight for this article would be about not using modal windows for showing error, success, or warning messages. I think it makes sense in that users won’t be able to reference what the modal window is referring to when it pops up this way. The article summarizes all the key parts a modal window should have which will be great to reference to if I decide to add one to my Mad Lib.

I also liked that the article pointed out that modal windows should be user-initiated since I personally dislike when a modal window pops up out of nowhere and often find myself closing it without reading. This would also be something I keep in mind if I ever add a modal window to my site.

Best Practices for Form Design

From this article, I learned various tips about how to design a form with the user's experience in mind. It showed how, if the process of filling out a form is simplified and straightforward, users were more inclined to finish filling out a form.

One area the article touches upon is how simplifying the design of your form can make the user feel less overwhelmed. This includes minimizing input fields, using a one-column layout, breaking a form down into multiple steps, auto-focusing on the first input field, providing a matching keyboard, and making sure the design is accessible to all.

The article also focuses on tips related specifically to the form's fields. These tips help make the experience of filling out a form feel less frustrating. Some of these tips include asking easy questions first, distinguishing optional fields, explaining formatting requirements, sizing field accordingly, and more.

While reading this article, I thought about all the digital forms I have had to fill out and could remember seeing most of these tips being implemented in them. The forms that did not follow these tips did seem clunky too me and more frustrating to use. Going forward, I will keep the tips I learned from this article in mind when designing my Mad Lib.
